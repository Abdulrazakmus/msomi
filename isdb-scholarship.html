Scholarship Overview

The Islamic Development Bank (ISDB) offers various scholarship programs to support the educational pursuits of students from member countries. The IDB scholarship programs aim to enhance human resources development in Islamic countries by providing opportunities for students to pursue higher education in specific fields of study.
Here are some key details about the IsDB scholarship program:

  1. Eligibility: The ISDB scholarships are primarily available to students from ISDB member countries who have been accepted into undergraduate, master's, or Ph.D. programs at reputable universities in their home countries or other member countries.
  2. Fields of Study: The ISDB scholarships cover a wide range of disciplines, including but not limited to science, technology, engineering, medicine, economics, finance, agriculture, and development-related fields.
  3. Coverage: The scholarship provides financial support to cover tuition fees, living expenses, accommodation, medical insurance, and economy class air tickets to and from the home country.
  4. Selection Process: The selection process for ISDB scholarships typically involves a competitive evaluation of applications, including academic performance, relevance of the field of study to the development needs of the student's home country, and potential impact on the community upon completion of the studies.
  5. Application Procedure: Interested students should visit the ISDB's official website or the scholarship section of their home country's IsDB representative office to access the application form and guidelines. The application process usually requires submitting academic transcripts, recommendation letters, a study plan, and other supporting documents.
It's important to note that the specific requirements and procedures for the IsDB scholarship program may vary over time. Therefore, it is recommended to visit the official IsDB website or contact the IsDB representative office in your country for the most up-to-date information regarding eligibility criteria, application deadlines, and application procedures.
